SPECIFICATIONS

AR500 STEEL

Providing AR500 steel sourced from US or Nordic region (where you can trust the hardness ratings). This provides complete rifle-rated ballistic steel coverage for all four sides of the Container Range and the roof, also considered here are any additional baffles required to guard penetrations for ventilation and to protect the lighting in the trailer. All AR500 steel panels will be cut to the correct size for direct installation into the container. All AR500 provided by ARI (Armored Range International) is provided with a mill certification that guarantees steel content.

ACOUSTIC BALLISTIC RUBBER COVERAGE

Our Range includes Ballistic Rubber Panels and/or Acoustic Panels for complete 360-degree ricochet protection. This will provide coverage for all 4 sides, ceiling, floor, and other areas where protection is required. Armored Range International recommends the use of acoustic Dura-panel at sidewalls in order cover acoustic and ballistic concerns with one material.

SOUND ATTENUATION

The range has acoustic and sound dampening materials throughout to minimize interior reverberation sound level and exterior sound transmission. Except for impulse gunshot sounds, background sound levels in the range will not exceed 85 decibels when the range is in operation. Hearing protection will have to be worn by all personnel within the shooting bay, in order to comply with OSHA noise exposure regulations, however hearing protection will not be required outside of the shooting bay.

VENTILATION

The ventilation and exhaust systems are a very important key element in conjunction with your bullet trap system to create and provide a clean and safe shooting environment for the shooter as well as range instructor’s and maintenance personnel. Air is supplied behind the firing line through a tuned/dampened perforated airwall at the back wall of the shooting bay. After flowing past the firing line at a velocity of ~75fpm, it is drawn down the length of the shooting bay by the negative pressure created by the return fan. Air is exhausted above the bullet trap. The range ventilation system is designed as a 100% purge-type system, meaning that 100% outside air is drawn in, heated or cooled (based on climatic design data), passed through the range and exhausted through filtration. The system, as designed, meets the requirements of NIOSH (U.S.) 76-130, as well as U.S. OSHA recommendations and U.S. EPA restrictions regarding discharge. The supply air is evenly drawn out through a continuous slot and at a nominal design value that is 110% greater than the supply airflow. This ensures that no contaminated air escapes to the outside through any cracks such as door jambs and sills. The range ventilation control panel is located near the entry door and in addition to providing start/stop functionality, displays the current range pressure and filter loading status.

LIGHTING AND ELECTRICITY

Much of ARI's Container Range is pre-wired to minimize on-site work and to prevent perforation of the ballistic envelope by electrical systems. ARI requires a single point connection from the customer’s electrician. The customer must provide electric utilities to supply the Container Range and associated equipment. All Range electrical sub-systems (ventilation, lighting, outlets, etc.) have separate circuits located inside a key-locked master circuit box installed in the main electrical box, located behind the bullet trap. All switches and outlets provided are Industrial standard quality, comply with the U.S. and Canadian National Electric Codes and carry UL and CSA ratings. Generally, lighting throughout the range is dimmable low-profile LED type. Outside every door to the outside of the range, there will be a “Range in Use” warning light. These are automatically illuminated anytime the ventilation system is energized. Low-profile, battery-powered, automatic emergency exit lighting is provided at the egress doors.

EXTERIOR FINISH AND INSULATION

The Container Range is finished with standard weather resistant exterior paint. Any chips or scratches caused during the shipping/installation process are touched up by hand on site. The space between the exterior siding/roof and the Armor ballistics shield is lined with a non-fiberglass, non-moulding insulating material made from 80% post-consumer materials, providing a thermal value of R-8. This both minimizes thermal transfer between the outside and interior spaces and helps prevent condensation build-up on the AR steel surfaces due to temperature imbalance. In addition, it helps to limit sound transmission from the range to the outside and is critical in maintaining that level at or below 85 dBA.

BULLET TRAPS

Armored Range International's Container Standard Range is supplied with a modified rubber block trap rated to safely defeat projectiles up to and including 7.62 x 51. The rubber in the trap defeats, stops and captures fired projectile by friction. This type of trap is widely used and has been the standard for clean indoor firing ranges for over 20 years. The rubber is treated with a calcium carbonate additive which increases flame resistance of the media. 1.6. For the purposes of this project we will use two types of bullet traps identified below. LOW VELOCITY: Bloc Bullet Trap this is ARI‘s standard rifle rated Bloc Bullet Trap for full width and height of back container wall (approximately 8’W x 8’H). Bloc Trap is ideal for a container-based range because it is a rifle-rated bullet trap that occupies less than 46cm of range depth. It is the only rifle-rated bullet trap option on the market that is less than 2.44M in depth. Dura-Blocs are stacked in place and are replaced modularly only at areas that require it. There is no need to replace complete sections of the bullet trap. Each Dura-Bloc is tested to encapsulate 5000 rounds. See additional product data attached. HIGH VELOCITY: 50Cal Bullet Trap Armored Range International offers the P-2000, the R-3600 and the 50Cal-95 bullet traps: Built specifically to be a clearing trap for .50 Cal and the .338 Lapua. These bullet traps come with a solid main body that will fit through a 3’ 0” door, enabling you to use standard door sizes to place it in the range. Once it is set in place the bolt-on extension plates are connected to the main body of the container to bring the trap out to its full height and width. All projectiles that enter the mouth of the trap are directed in to the rear vertical cylindrical bullet catch that decelerates the bullet until it loses its inertia and fails into the collection container under the back body of the trap. No other media such as rubber, sand or water are used in this passive style of trap. Therefore, you have nothing to sift , separate or “mine” out or your bullet trap system to collect the lead. ARI bullet traps are of a vertical design. Along with using the advantage of gravity to slow and collect the bullets, this unique system allows for a very important mating and connection to the two inside walls of your range envelope that houses the bullet traps. This vertical system is far superior to horizontal designs when it comes to containing rounds that strike these inside walls and to keep bullets from getting around the trap and striking the rear wall behind the bullet traps.
